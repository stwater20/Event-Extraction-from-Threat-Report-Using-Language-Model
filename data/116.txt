On Tuesday 2017-01-03, BleepingComputer published an article about "Merry X-Mas Ransomware". | O
This ransomware was first seen by people like @PolarToffee, @dvk01uk, and @Techhelplistcom. | O
Merry X-Mas Ransomware was first reported as distributed through malicious spam (malspam) disguised as FTC consumer complaints. | Phishing
By Sunday 2017-01-08, I saw an updated version of the Merry X-Mas Ransomware distributed through malspam disguised as court attendance notifications. | Phishing
It seemed odd to find Christmas-themed ransomware two weeks after Christmas; however, Orthodox Christian communities celebrate Christmas on January 7th. | O
Ultimately, such Christmas-themed ransomware isn't odd if it's from a Russian actor. | O
With that in mind, let's review the characteristics of Sunday's Merry X-Mas ransomware. | O

The malspam was a fake notification to appear in court. | O
Email headers indicate the sender's address was spoofed, and the email came from a cloudapp.net domain associated with Microsoft. | Phishing
The zip archive contained a Microsoft Word document with a malicious macro. | O
If macros were enabled on the Word document, it downloaded and executed the ransomware. | O
